# D431 — Moffatt v. Air Canada (2024) liability gap

**Status:** open
**Priority:** P2
**Filed:** 2026-04-06

## Problem
Moffatt v. Air Canada (2024) confirmed that client businesses are fully liable for AI errors — wrong prices quoted, wrong availability confirmed, wrong commitments made. unmissed has no verbal confirmation step before agent commitments, no E&O advisory in onboarding, and no ToS clause offloading liability to the client business.

## Root Cause
Legal exposure never addressed in product design.

## Fix
(1) Add a clause to unmissed ToS stating the client business is responsible for all commitments made by their AI agent, (2) Add an advisory in onboarding step 6 ("Your agent speaks on your behalf — review its settings carefully"), (3) For niches where price quotes or bookings are made, add a verbal confirmation step to FORBIDDEN_EXTRA: "NEVER commit to a specific price or availability without first saying 'let me confirm that with the team and they'll call you right back.'"

## Notes
Risk is low per-call but could be high-profile if a client's agent causes a significant financial dispute. Address ToS first, then prompt hardening.
