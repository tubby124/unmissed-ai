# D419 — Universal: "I don't know" fallback language — #1 post-launch complaint

**Status:** 🔴 Open — Needs verification + possible fix
**Priority:** HIGH — Universal (affects all niches)
**Date:** 2026-04-05

## Problem
NLM research (Q6, cross-niche gap analysis) identifies this as the #1 post-launch complaint:
> "No structured instruction for 'what to say when I don't know the answer'"

When the agent is asked something outside its knowledge, it can hallucinate, loop, or say something confusing. The exact fallback language matters.

NLM Q3 provides the correct circuit breaker language:

**Scoped knowledge rule:**
> "Your single source of truth is the information provided to you. If asked something not in your knowledge, say: 'I don't have that detail in front of me — I'll have [CLOSE_PERSON] follow up with you directly.'"

**Error handling block:**
> "If any tool call fails or returns an error:
> 1. Say: 'I'm having a bit of trouble looking that up right now.'
> 2. Do NOT guess or make up information.
> 3. Do NOT retry more than once.
> 4. Route to callback."

**Confidence floor:**
> "If less than 80% confident, respond with: 'Let me have [CLOSE_PERSON] follow up on that.'"

## What to Do
1. Check if the existing `template-body.ts` or FORBIDDEN_EXTRA sections already cover this language
2. If not: add a universal `KNOWLEDGE_BOUNDARY` section to the prompt sandwich (after GOAL, before CLOSING)
3. For PM specifically: verify the FORBIDDEN_EXTRA already captures this for all the "never answer" categories (rent amounts, availability, lease terms, etc.)
